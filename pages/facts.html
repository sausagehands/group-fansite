Top 5 Cool Sloth Facts

  1. They are shockingly good swimmers: Even though sloths are slow on land, they’re three times faster in water. They use a kind of gentle breaststroke and can hold their breath for up to 40 minutes by slowing their metabolism.
  2. They have a whole ecosystem living in their fur: Sloth fur hosts algae, beetles, fungi, and moths—some of which live only on sloths. The algae tint their fur green for camouflage, and in return, the sloth gets nutrients when grooming. It’s like a tiny moving rainforest!
  3. Their digestion is ridiculously slow: It can take a sloth 7–30 days to digest a single leaf. Because of this slow process, up to one-third of their body weight can be made up of food sitting in their stomach.
  4. They only poop once a week—and it’s dangerous: Sloths climb down from their trees to poop, which makes them vulnerable to predators. Scientists call it a “poop run,” though it’s more like a slow shuffle. No one knows exactly why they risk it, but it might help maintain their fur ecosystem.
  5. They have extra neck vertebrae: Most mammals have seven neck vertebrae—sloths can have eight or nine. This lets them rotate their heads up to 270°, owl-style, so they can watch for predators without moving their bodies.
