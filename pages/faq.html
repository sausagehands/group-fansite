FAQS

General Information

What exactly is a sloth?

Sloths are tree-dwelling mammals native to Central and South America. There are two main types — two-toed and three-toed sloths — named for the number of claws on their front limbs.

How long do sloths live?

Wild sloths live around 20–30 years, but in captivity they can reach their 40s.

What’s the difference between two-toed and three-toed sloths?

Two-toed sloths are larger, nocturnal, and slightly faster. Three-toed sloths are smaller, active in the daytime, and have the signature “smiling” face.

Behavior & Lifestyle

Why do sloths move so slowly?

Their slow movement conserves energy and helps them stay hidden from predators. Their leaf-based diet provides very little energy, so they move only when necessary.

Do sloths really sleep all day?

Wild sloths sleep about 10 hours a day, not the rumored 20. The rest of the time, they’re quietly eating or hanging from branches.

How often do sloths come down from the trees?

Only about once a week — usually to poop. The rest of their lives happen in the trees.

Do sloths ever touch the ground?

Rarely. When they do, they move awkwardly by dragging themselves since their bodies are built for hanging, not walking.

How do baby sloths learn to climb?

Baby sloths cling to their mothers for months, watching and mimicking her movements until they can climb independently.

Diet & Digestion

What do sloths eat?

They’re folivores — meaning they eat mostly leaves, twigs, and fruit. Their slow metabolism means they need very little food.

Why is their digestion so slow?

Their multi-chambered stomachs break down tough plant material very slowly. It can take up to a month to digest one meal.

How do sloths get water?

They rarely drink directly; instead, they absorb moisture from the leaves and morning dew they eat.

Habitat & Adaptations

Why do sloths have green fur sometimes?

Algae and fungi grow on their fur, giving them a greenish tint that helps camouflage them. These organisms even form a tiny ecosystem unique to sloths.

Are sloths good swimmers?

Yes! Sloths can swim three times faster than they move on land and hold their breath for up to 40 minutes.

How do sloths defend themselves?

Their best defense is staying still and blending in. Camouflage and silence keep them safe from predators like jaguars and harpy eagles.

Conservation & Interaction

Are sloths endangered?

Some species are. The Pygmy three-toed sloth, found only on a small Panamanian island, is critically endangered due to habitat loss.

Can sloths be pets?

No — sloths are wild animals that need specific diets and climates. They become easily stressed and unhealthy in captivity.

What are their main predators?

Jaguars, ocelots, harpy eagles, and anacondas are among the few predators that can catch a sloth if it moves too much.
